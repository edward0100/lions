Facts About Lions

  • Lions are the only cats that live in groups, which are called prides.
  • A group of lions usually includes related females, their offspring, and a small number of adult males.
  • Lions are carnivorous and primarily prey on large herbivores such as zebras, wildebeests, and buffalo.
  • Male lions are easily recognized by their manes, which can range from blonde to black in color.
  • Lions can run at speeds of up to 50 miles per hour for short distances.
  • Despite their fearsome reputation, lions spend much of their time resting and can sleep for up to 20 hours a day.
  • The lion population has declined by more than 40% over the past two decades due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.
